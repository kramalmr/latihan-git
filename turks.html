Turks

The Turks (Turkish: T rkl r) are a nation and ethnic group originally from Central Asia, who speak Turkic languages and share, to a certain extent, a common cultural and historical heritage.

They have historically been a major influence on the development of civilizations in the regions they have inhabited, contributing to the cultural, political, and economic landscapes.

The Turkic peoples have been known to have a history of nomadic lifestyles, and have developed a number of architectural styles, such as yurts, and have a tradition of mythology and literature.

The diverse cultural practices of the Turks are reflected in their music, dance, and traditional clothing, which vary significantly across different regions and communities.

The Turks have a long history of Islamic influence, with many of the Ottoman Empire's subjects being Muslim.

The Turks have played a significant role in the history of the Middle East and Eastern Europe, with the Ottoman Empire being one of the most powerful states in the world during its peak. Their legacy can be seen in the architectural, cultural, and culinary influences that persist today.

The modern Republic of Turkey is a secular parliamentary democracy with a diverse economy, a member of the NATO alliance, and a candidate to join the European Union.
